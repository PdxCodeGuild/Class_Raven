Seneca the Younger

Lucius Annaeus Seneca the Younger (/ˈsɛnɪkə/; c. 4 BC – AD 65), usually known as Seneca, was a Roman Stoic philosopher, statesman, dramatist, and in one work, satirist, from the post-Augustan age of Latin literature (credit wikipedia - link below)

Seneca's Fast Facts:

Where He's Been

  • Born in 4BCE in Cordoba, Hispania (modern day Spain)
  • Shortly after birth, he moved to Rome where he was raised and educated in philosophy and rhetoric
  • In the year 41 CE, he was exiled to the island of Corsica by the Roman Emporer Claudius
  • Seneca returned to Rome in the year 49 to become the tutor to the New Emporer Nero

What Seneca Accomplished

  • Seneca contributed greatly to the school of Stocism. This Hellenistic philosophical idealology was started in the 3rd century BCE. It extols virtue ethics based on logic and living in accordance with nature.
  • Over a dozen philosophical essays, 120 letters on morals, and nine tragedies
  • In addition to his work with the Roman Goverment and contributions as a philosopher, Seneca is attributed as an author of up to 10 plays

Badass Quotes by Seneca:

  • We suffer more often in imagination than in reality
  • All cruelty springs from weakness
  • Luck is what happens when preparation meets opportunity.
  • True happiness is to enjoy the present, without anxious dependence upon the future, not to amuse ourselves with either hopes or fears but to rest satisfied with what we have, which is sufficient, for he that is so, wants nothing.
