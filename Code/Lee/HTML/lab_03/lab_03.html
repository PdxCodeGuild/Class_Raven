Emergency Rooms should ideally be used for only serious and potentially life threatening medical conditions.

If you have a potentially life threatening condition, please go straight to your nearest Emergency Room (ER). These conditions are best evaluated at the ER as they may require rapid or advanced treatment. Examples include:

- Chest pain, significant difficulty breathing, severe and/or uncontrolled bleeding, signs of a stroke including: weakness/numbness on one side, slurred speech.

Urgent Care - Urgent care centers are same-day, walk-in clinics that can treat medical problems not considered true emergencies.
If you are experiencing a non-emergent medical condition that cannot wait until the next available appointment at the office, then you should consider going to Urgent Care.

Examples include:
- Cold symptoms, nausea, vomiting, and / or diarrhea, mild asthma exacerbation, rashes, dehydration, sprains and strains, small cuts that may require stitches.

Our physicians at HPCA can help decide the best course of action if you’re ever unsure of when or where to seek medical care. Advance care planning (ACP) is making decisions about the care you would want to receive if you become unable to speak for yourself. ACP documents include an Advanced Health Care Directive (AHCD) and a Physician's Orders for Life Sustaining Treatment (POLST).

Our office offers various resources to help you with ACP:
 - Kokua Mau's AHCD Template
 - Queen’s Medical Center’s Free ACP Clinic: call 808-691-7735 for more information
 - My ACP Decisions Videos: Inquire about a prescription at your next visit to get access to ACP videos

Queens Clinically Integrated Physicians Network (QCIPN) consists of physicians and health care professions that work to create the best clinical practices to make Hawaii healthier. This network currently represents over 975 physicians across five islands.

QCIPN's Services:
 - Educational workshops and symposiums (in-person and online)
 - In-home assessments
 - Care coordination
 - Assistance with Advanced Care Planning
 - Mental health consultations
 - Monthly newsletters

  Give our office a call or tell us about your interest in QCIPN at your next appointment.
